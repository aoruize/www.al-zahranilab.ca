Functional Genomics Research Lab

At The University of Toronto — Donnelly Centre for Cellular and Biomolecular Research

Research Focus

Our Science

We develop and apply high-throughput in vivo functional genomics tools to study how large-scale chromosomal alterations drive tumourigenesis and can be leveraged as a therapeutic vulnerability in cancer.

Functional Genomics Tools

Developing CRISPR-based multiplexing approaches to study the causes and consequences of genetic and transcriptional heterogeneity in cancer.

Mouse Models of Disease

Building new mouse models of cancer amenable to functional interrogation through direct in vivo genetic manipulation of cells within their native environment.

Aneuploidy in Cancer

Combining functional genomics tools with mouse models to investigate how chromosomal and transcriptional heterogeneities drive cancer and can be targeted therapeutically.
